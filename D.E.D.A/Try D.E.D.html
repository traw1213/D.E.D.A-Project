Are you under the influence of the algorithms? Ask yourself the following Question.

  • Am I Seeing a Pattern?Do I notice that the content I'm seeing online seems to follow a specific theme or narrative, even across different platforms?
  • Emotional Responses:Do I feel unusually strong emotions (positive or negative) after spending time on a particular platform or after seeing specific types of content?
  • Time Perception:Do I lose track of time when I'm online? Do I spend more time than I intended to?
  • Comparison with Others:Do I often compare my life, achievements, or appearance to those I see online? Does it affect my self-worth?
  • Change in Beliefs:Have my beliefs or opinions shifted recently? Can I trace back these changes to specific content or sources online?
  • Echo Chambers:Am I only exposed to one perspective or type of content? Do I find it hard to understand or accept opposing views?
  • Content Dependency:Do I feel the need to constantly check certain apps or platforms? Do I feel anxious if I don't?
  • Real vs. Online:Do I find it hard to differentiate between what's a widespread belief in the real world versus what's commonly presented to me online?
  • External Validations:Do I seek validation through likes, comments, shares, or other digital engagements? Does my mood depend on it?
  • Learning Sources:Do I rely solely on one platform or source for information, especially on critical topics?
  • Physical Health:Do I experience physical symptoms like eye strain, headaches, or disrupted sleep after prolonged online sessions?
  • Content Recall:Can I recall the last five pieces of content I consumed? Were they diverse, or did they follow a specific theme?
  • Active vs. Passive Consumption:Do I actively seek out content, or do I mostly consume what's fed to me by algorithms?
  • Social Interactions:Do I find myself discussing or debating online topics frequently in real-life interactions? Do they influence my perception of others?
  • Desire for Change:Have I ever felt the need to change something about myself (beliefs, appearance, lifestyle) based on the content I've consumed?

About D.E.D.A: Bringing Awarness through Sensory Integration Therapy.

  • Colors: Chromotherapy or Color Therapy - This therapy believes that colors can influence our emotions and well-being. Different colors are said to evoke different responses in people.
  • Sequences: This could refer to a structured or systematic approach to therapy, where sequences or patterns are used to evoke certain responses or behaviors.
  • Sounds: Sound Therapy or Music Therapy - This involves the use of music or specific sounds to improve health or functional outcomes. It can evoke memories, emotions, and even physical responses.
  • Bibliotherapy - This is the use of reading materials recommended by therapists to help clients understand and solve their problems. Reading can offer insights, promote self-awareness, and provide comfort. It can be a way for individuals to recognize their own experiences and feelings in the narratives of others, leading to a sense of validation and understanding. Bibliotherapy can be used in conjunction with traditional talk therapy or as a standalone approach, depending on the individual's needs and the therapist's recommendations.
  • Frequencies: Binaural Beats or Brainwave Entrainment - This is a method where sounds of two different frequencies are played in each ear, and the brain perceives a third tone that is the mathematical difference between the two. It's believed to enhance relaxation, meditation, stress reduction, pain management, improved sleep, and even improved learning.
